Introduction

Our report breaks down some NBA data from the 2025 season. Given the variation in the number of minutes a player gets each game, we are looking at statistics per-36 minutes. These numbers are not equivalent to the box score numbers that same player would tally in an actual game. Data from this report are based on numbers through Mar 07, 2025.

Conclusion

Our report uses NBA data to assess performance in different ways. We looked at a combined score of (points + assists + rebounds), a plot of age vs. rank, and then a closer look at points for some specific players. One limitation of this report is that per-36 minute stats do not always approximate a player’s true box score numbers. The best players often play over closer to 40 minutes per game, so their stats may appear less impressive. On the opposite end, fringe rotation players who get very few minutes will have their stats inflated. Nevertheless, per-36 minute numbers are a good proxy for player performance.
